# Session: Sarah (UX) — Gabriele Interview Answers

## Raw Answers — Italian (verbatim, as received)

**1 — Posto di lavoro**
Lavoro in una stanza dell'appartamento ricavata.
Quando lavoro vedo esclusivamente quello che sto facendo. Sono assorto completamente a quello che sto facendo.

**2 — Radici**
No, è un hobby esclusivamente mio.

**3 — Come si comincia un presepe**
Il presepe è una libera espressione di chi lo vuole costruire.
In base all'idea che si ha si trova il materiale.

**4 — Materiale e difficoltà**
Il materiale primario con cui lavoro è cartone nelle varie misure di spessore.
Ricoperto di stucco e colorato.
La parte più difficile è l'illuminazione. Con la parte elettrica ho dei problemi.

**5 — Il pezzo a cui è più legato**
Man mano che costruisco aumenta la mia esperienza e trovo sempre nuove tecniche, quindi diciamo che è l'ultima creazione è sempre quella dove sono più soddisfatto e legato.

**6 — Ha mai fatto pezzi per qualcuno**
Non ho mai fatto pezzi per qualcuno anche se mi piacerebbe tantissimo.

**7 — Cosa spera che si senta guardando il suo presepe**
Quando qualcuno guarda il mio presepe spero sempre che non trovi errori, e che gli dia il senso della festa.

**8 — Rapporto con la tradizione**
Il presepe va sempre reinterpretato, ed è per questo che sopravvive da molto tempo.

**10 — Perché vale la pena averne uno**
Così come l'albero di natale anche il presepe è l'espressione del periodo natalizio.

*(Domanda 9 non pervenuta — non richiesta dall'intervistato)*

## Sarah's Analysis — What This Tells Us

### The person behind the page

Gabriele is not what someone might expect from an artisan with a website. He:
- Works in a converted room of his apartment — not a romantic workshop, but a real, personal space
- Is completely self-taught — this is entirely his story, no inherited tradition
- Has **never made a piece for someone** — the site is his first step toward that

That last point is the emotional core of the page. He says "mi piacerebbe tantissimo" — he would love it very much. The site exists because he wants to share what he makes. That is the story.

### What to use, and where

**For the page opening / hero text:**
Answer 1 is intimate and unusual. "Sono assorto completamente" — he disappears into the work. This is the quality of attention that makes handmade things different. Don't open with biography; open with the act of making.

**For the materials section:**
Answer 4 is deceptively simple. Cardboard, plaster, paint. The transformation from humble materials to the finished piece is the craft. This contrast is visually interesting too — raw cardboard next to a finished, lit presepe. If we can get a photo of the material + the result, that spread would be powerful.

The lighting struggle is authentic and charming. It humanises him — he is a craftsman, not an electrician. Don't hide it; it's what makes him real.

**For the "philosophy" section:**
Answer 3 and Answer 8 together form his creative manifesto:
- "Il presepe è una libera espressione di chi lo vuole costruire" — freedom
- "Va sempre reinterpretato, ed è per questo che sopravvive" — tradition needs reinterpretation to survive

These are strong, quotable lines. They should appear as pull quotes, not buried in prose.

**For the closing / call to action:**
Answer 6 is the most important answer on the page. He has never made for someone. He would love to. The CTA ("Sono interessato" → Contact) is not commercial pressure — it is an invitation to be the first. That framing changes the energy of the page entirely.

Answer 10 is honest but thin — "like the Christmas tree." It works as an ending observation but should not be the closing statement of the page. The closing statement should be the invitation, not the explanation.

### Answers that need a follow-up (optional — not blocking)

- **Answer 1**: I'd love a physical detail — what does he see when he looks up from the work? A window, a wall, his tools? One image would anchor the page.
- **Answer 7**: "Che non trovi errori" — this is beautifully humble. A follow-up: *what would an error be, for him?* That answer could be a standout quote.
- **Answer 10**: Thin. If there is a second conversation, push here: *perché il presepe artigianale e non quello comprato?* The handmade distinction is what the site sells.

None of these are blockers for the page.

## Content Brief for Helios

**Page:** Chi sono / The Maker / Der Meister
**Ticket:** ISS-007 (this session closes the UX/research phase; content writing is Helios's scope)
**Tone:** Warm, intimate, first person where possible. Not a CV. Not a sales page. A portrait.

**Suggested page structure:**

1. **Opening** — the act of making. Draw from Answer 1. Not "Gabriele is an artisan from..." — start inside the room, inside the work.

2. **The materials** — cardboard, plaster, paint. Answer 4. The transformation. Include the lighting difficulty — it's honest and human.

3. **The philosophy** — two pull quotes from Answers 3 and 8. "Libera espressione" and "va sempre reinterpretato." Short connecting prose.

4. **The latest piece** — Answer 5. He is always most attached to the last one. This is a beautiful way to say: he never stops growing.

5. **The invitation** — Answer 6. He has never made for someone. He would love to. This is the bridge to the CTA. The Contact link is not a button — it is the answer to his unspoken question.

**Languages:** IT is the source. EN and DE translations should preserve rhythm and directness — do not over-polish. Gabriele speaks plainly; the English and German should too.

**Image needs** (to flag for Owner / Gabriele):
- Portrait: hands visible, ideally at work — not posed
- Workshop/room: the actual space where he works
- Materials: raw cardboard next to a finished piece if possible
- A finished, lit presepe — the lighting being the hardest part makes this the culmination image
